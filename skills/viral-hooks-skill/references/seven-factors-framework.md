# The 7 Factors of Viral Short-Form Content

Every high-performing short-form video consists of seven factors. Use this framework to go from blank page to fully optimized short.

> These factors are flexible in order — many creators pick hooks before topic, or choose format before scripting. Adapt to whatever flow works best.

## Factor 1: Topic

**What it is**: The subject of the video.

**How to choose**:
1. Write out your main broad niche (fitness, nutrition, business, sales, fashion, beauty, etc.)
2. List 5-7 subniches under that niche
   - Example (fitness): lifting, cardio, nutrition, protein, sleep, getting a six-pack
3. Pick ONE subniche as the topic for this video

**When generating content for users**: Always confirm their niche first, then help them identify subniches. Each piece of content should target one specific subniche — never the broad niche itself.

## Factor 2: Hooks (Verbal, Written, Visual)

Every short needs THREE hooks working together:
- **Verbal hook**: What the viewer HEARS in the first 3 seconds
- **Written hook**: What the viewer READS in the first 3 seconds (on-screen text)
- **Visual hook**: What the viewer SEES in the first 3 seconds (setting, action, props)

### The 5x Outlier Rule

Never guess at hooks. Research proven outlier hooks — videos from small-to-mid-size creators that got 5x more views than their follower count. If it already went viral, it has a better chance of going viral again.

### Three Research Methods for Finding Hooks

**Method 1 — Creator Research**:
- Go to the platform's explore/discover page
- Search the video topic
- Find creators in the niche
- Identify their top 5-10 most viral videos
- Save any that follow the 5x rule

**Method 2 — Keyword Research**:
- Search the video topic on the platform
- Browse the results (Reels tab, etc.)
- Save videos that follow the 5x rule
- Try different keyword variations to find more

**Method 3 — Algorithm Recommendations**:
- After doing Methods 1 and 2, scroll the recommendations feed
- The algorithm will surface related content based on your research activity
- This surfaces "hidden outlier gems" that don't appear in keyword or creator searches
- Save any 5x rule videos

### Hook Application

When generating hooks for users:
- Pull from the hooks-library.md templates AND apply the outlier research mindset
- All three hook types (verbal, written, visual) should be addressed
- Written hooks should be short, punchy, and create curiosity
- Visual hook suggestions should describe what the viewer sees (setting, action, expression, prop)

## Factor 3: Value

**What it is**: The meat of the video — the educational takeaway.

### Two Sources of Value

1. **Personal knowledge/experience** — Preferred. This is what makes the creator authentic and authoritative. If someone is an expert, they should lead with their own insights.
2. **External informational sources** — Studies, articles, expert references. Not bad, but always credit the source.

Most content should mix both, leaning toward personal expertise.

### Fluffy Value vs. Measurable Value

| Fluffy Value (BAD) | Measurable Value (GOOD) |
|---|---|
| Unclear, vague | Specific and detailed |
| No actionable takeaway | Viewer knows exactly what to do next |
| Generic advice | Concrete numbers, steps, or frameworks |
| "Just work harder" | "Do 3 sets of 8-12 reps at 70% 1RM, 3x per week" |

**Rule**: Every video must give the viewer something specific that makes their life better. If they can't act on it after watching, the value is fluffy.

### Value Examples (Fitness Niche)

- Breaking down a study comparing exercise variations — which is most effective
- Sharing a full day of eating for under $8 — budget muscle-building
- Five steps to test your dead hang time — self-assessment framework
- Rating training splits from personal experience — tier list with reasoning

**When generating content for users**: Always push for measurable value. Ask "what specific thing will the viewer be able to DO after watching this?" If the answer is vague, make it more concrete.

## Factor 4: Script Angle

**What it is**: The direction/approach the video takes. One topic can become 7+ videos by changing the angle.

### The 7 Main Angles

| # | Angle | Description | Example (Topic: How to Get Abs) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Tutorial** | Step-by-step process, framework, acronym | "My 4-step ab routine that took me from 20% to 12% body fat" |
| 2 | **Comparison** | Compare actions, methods, products | "Crunches vs. planks — which actually builds visible abs faster?" |
| 3 | **Myth Bust / Common Mistake** | Break a common belief or debunk a mistake | "The #1 ab exercise everyone does is actually destroying your spine" |
| 4 | **Do's vs. Don'ts** | What NOT to do vs. what TO do | "Stop doing this if you want abs (and do this instead)" |
| 5 | **Educational Tip/Hack** | One-off niche tip, hack, or lesson | "The 30-second ab trick that changed everything for me" |
| 6 | **Transformation** | Before/after personal or client result | "How my client got visible abs in 90 days — here's exactly what we did" |
| 7 | **Challenge** | Attempting to achieve a specific result | "I tried getting a six-pack in 30 days — here's what happened" |

### Script Approach

After choosing an angle, decide on the delivery style:
- **Educational**: Straightforward teaching — "Here's what you need to know"
- **Storytelling**: Narrative framing — "Let me tell you about the time I..."

Both work. Storytelling tends to have higher retention; educational tends to have higher save rates.

**When generating content for users**: Suggest 2-3 different angles for the same topic to show versatility. This also helps with batch content — same topic, different angles = a week of content.

## Factor 5: CTA (Call to Action)

**What it is**: What you tell the viewer to do at the end.

### Two Main CTA Goals

1. **Build following / personal brand**: Direct people to follow, save, share
2. **Drive leads**: Send people to a freebie, product, service, or link

### CTA Best Practices

- Be direct and specific about what to do
- For link-based CTAs: Use comment-triggered automation (e.g., ManyChat) instead of "link in bio"
  - Viewer comments a keyword — gets DM'd the link automatically
  - Keywords should be under 5-7 letters
- Match the CTA to the content's goal — don't always sell; mostly build relationship

### CTA Templates by Goal

**Following/Brand Building**:
- "Follow for more [niche] tips that actually work"
- "Save this for later — you'll need it"
- "Tag someone who needs to hear this"
- "Follow if you want to see part 2"

**Lead Generation**:
- "Comment [KEYWORD] and I'll send you the free [resource]"
- "Comment [KEYWORD] to get the full [framework/template/guide]"
- "I made a free [resource] that breaks all of this down — comment [KEYWORD] and I'll DM it to you"

**When generating content for users**: Always include a CTA suggestion. Ask what their goal is (grow following vs. drive leads) if not clear. Default to brand-building CTAs unless they have a product/service to promote.

## Factor 6: Format

**What it is**: The engaging way the video is filmed. Talking-head-only is outdated — the filming format directly impacts watch time.

### 11 Proven Video Formats

| # | Format | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Talking Back & Forth** | Creator plays both the "smart" and "dumb" character, teaching through dialogue |
| 2 | **Visual/Props** | Physical props or visuals used to demonstrate a concept |
| 3 | **Voiceover** | Images/video clips on screen with voice narration over them |
| 4 | **Multitasking** | Teaching while doing something unrelated (cooking, cleaning, makeup, walking) |
| 5 | **Setting Change** | Location changes throughout the video to maintain visual interest |
| 6 | **Shot/Angle Change** | New camera angle every 2 seconds — high energy, fast-paced |
| 7 | **Clone** | Creator doubled on screen comparing two things side-by-side |
| 8 | **Whiteboard** | Using a whiteboard or writing surface to explain a concept |
| 9 | **Q&A** | Someone behind camera asks questions; one or multiple people answer on camera |
| 10 | **Green Screen** | Videos/images playing behind the creator, correlating with what they're saying |
| 11 | **Reaction** | React to a mega-viral video in the niche, then give expert opinion |

### Format Selection Tips

- Choose the format BEFORE or AFTER scripting — either works, but format choice may require script adjustments
- Some formats pair naturally with certain angles (tutorial — whiteboard; transformation — visual/props; myth bust — reaction)
- Mixing formats across a content calendar keeps the feed dynamic

**When generating content for users**: Suggest 1-2 formats that pair well with the chosen angle. Include brief filming notes (e.g., "film in 2-3 different locations" or "use a whiteboard and marker for this one").

## Factor 7: Editing Style

**What it is**: The consistent visual identity across all your videos — text, colors, visuals, and music.

### Three Elements to Lock In

**1. Colors** — Choose 2-3 main colors
- Use these consistently across all text, graphics, and overlays
- Can follow color theory rules or just pick what fits the brand

**2. Fonts** — Choose 2-3 fonts
- **Primary font**: Bold, attention-grabbing — used for written hooks and key text
- **Secondary font**: Clean, readable — used for regular captions/subtitles
- **Accent font** (optional): Used sparingly for annotations, callouts, or emphasis

**3. Visuals** — Choose 2-3 consistent visual types used throughout videos
- Screenshots, screen recordings, B-roll, images, PNGs, charts, graphs, memes, etc.
- Sprinkle these throughout every video for visual variety

### Why This Matters

Pick these three elements once, then apply them to every video. This creates a recognizable, branded editing style that viewers associate with the creator.

**When generating content for users**: If they haven't established an editing style, suggest one based on their niche and personality. Include color hex codes, font name suggestions, and visual type recommendations.

## Key Principles (From the Framework)

1. **Never sacrifice integrity for views** — Don't post trends, aesthetic content, or fluffy content just for short-term virality. Build with valuable, educational, personal, and authentic content.
2. **Quality followers > quantity** — Viral off of substance gets followers who actually care, engage, and convert. Viral off of trends gets hollow numbers.
3. **Patience is non-negotiable** — First big wins can come in a week or take months. The only way to fail is to stop posting.
4. **Consistency is the multiplier** — Every successful creator went through the pain of growth. Keep posting, keep refining.
5. **Avoid fluffy content** — No memes, useless trends, or content that doesn't help the viewer. Every post should make someone's life measurably better.
